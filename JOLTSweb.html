Jovian Open Lunar Trade Settlement

Mission: JOLT-3

Mission OverView:

Congratulations! As one of the top candidates during training you have been selected as the Captain of the Hercales deep space vessal to complete the JOLT-3 mission. We expect that you will read through the mission briefing carefully and join us for further planning at PlanEX launch facility at 06:00 for further training and planning. We cannot stress that the training you will be subjected to is incredibly dangerous, but if there was any doubt you couldn't complete the tasks at hand you would not have been selected for the mission. Very few are qualified the following for this mission profile so we expect you to know the vital role you play as part of the Hercales crew. Keep this in mind as you read through this overview.

As you are well aware, PlanEX Inc has been interested in the Jovian system for a long time but until now the fuel and energy requirements demanded for the mission have been too great for such a high mass. It has only been thanks to a breakthrough in ion-drive technology that allows a manned mission to Jupiter to be more pheasible. The opportunity of having a Jovian settlement so early is too great of an opportunity to let pass by.

Resource extraction is the primary objective of the settlement we hope to establish. Current analysis suggests that IO has been increasing in density for several millenia, thus increasing the probability of finding high quantities of heavy metals at the surface and Callisto, Europa, and Ganymede have near unlimited quantities of water ice extractable for habititation or rocket fuel. All moons of the Jovian system have a lesser surface gravity than that of moon making surface to orbit transport. We can also reasonably conclude that the number of gravitational assists from probes will continue to increase giving us a consistant revenue stream.

Your mission (JoLT-3) is to set up a habitation and resupply station on Europa for future Jovian missions. It is imperitive that we have access to substantial amounts of water for the IO colony for both habitation and radiation shielding from Jupiter's magnetosphere. Europa has been chosen for both it's proximity and it's wide array of H2O states being relatively close to the surface.

As Captain and Astronautic specialist it is your job to know every system and every component on the ship. All crew members will be trained for a wide variety of hazards and emergency situations. As captain it is your job to know all vehichle and equipment with uncanny precision. This includes both hardware and software. Hercales will be your home for nearly 2 years and any malfunction or component failure could have unforseen consequences, including but not limited too the loss of life of the entire crew.

Why Start With Europa

  • In close proximity to IO
  • Has exotic forms of H2O close to the surface
  • Ice content requires less refining than Callisto and Ganymede
  • Shallow spots may allow us to pump subsurface ocean directly to the surface

Mission Profile:

History

In 2035 there was a breakthrough in ion propulsion techniques that allowed us to get to the Martian colonies in under a month. We deployed it on one of our Chariot craft unmanned for testing and it went unexpectedly smoothly and after we started an assembly line of our Chariot craft with a couple more optimizations that were soon called the Pegasus lineup. After this we started seeing if we could create a larger engine series purposed for a much more massive ship. It was long until our physics department determined that it would be able deliver a habitation transport grade freighter to Jupiter in under 2 years. It was then that project JOLT was born.

JOLT-1 was launched in 2040 and was a reconiscense craft meant to determine the pheasablity of a manned Jovian mission. It was meant to answer a whole host of unanswered questions such as... What resources are easily accesible on the Jovian moons? How deep are the ice shells of the galilean moons? How much radiation protection is needed from Jupiter's magnetosphere? Are there any other hazards on the Jovian enviornment? IO proved to be rich of high value minerals on it's surface and Europa had a couple of icesheets that were thinner than 500 meters. There was also research conducted on Ganymede's magnetosphere to see how it interacted with the Jovian magnetosphere. The Midas probe is still orbiting Jupiter updating our knowledge on the characteristics of these satelites.

Soon after we recieved the data of how shallow some ice sheets were on Europa's surface we started designing JOLT-2. JOLT-2 is currently laying the groundworks for you and the crew as we speak. It did another radiation test on it's way there and has inspired the design of the Hercales radiation shield. It has delivered cargo and supplies for you in preparation for your arrival. A habitat is currently being constructed by Ice-ThreeD. Your habitat will be created long before your arrival.
